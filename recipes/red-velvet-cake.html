Red Velvet Cake
thegratefulgirlcooks.com

Ingredients:

  • Cake:
  • 1 1/2 cups sugar
  • 1/2 cup Crisco
  • 2 oz red food coloring
  • 2 eggs - room temperature
  • 2 cups flour - sifted
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 TBSP unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 TBSP vanilla extract
  • 1 TBSP white vinegar
  • Frosting:
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 1/2 TBSP cornstarch
  • pinch salt
  • 1/2 cup Crisco
  • 1/2 cup butter - softened
  • 2/3 cup sugar
  • 1 2/3 cups powdered sugar - divided
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F and grease two 9-inch round cake pans or one 9x13-inch pan.
  2. Using an electric mixer or stand mixer, cream the sugar and Crisco together until well combined. Add the red food coloring and eggs.
  3. Continue mixing until the batter is deep red in color and creamy in texture.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk the flour, salt, and cocoa powder until well combined. Add this to the batter and mix on low speed until fully incorporated.
  5. In another bowl, combine the buttermilk and baking soda. Fold the mixture into the cake batter.
  6. Stir in the vanilla and vinegar until combined.
  7. If using two round cake pans, divide the batter evenly between the pans.
  8. Pour the batter into the prepared pan(s) and bake on the middle rack for about 25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Transfer pans to a wire rack to cool.
  9. While the cake is baking, add the milk, cornstarch, and salt to a medium saucepan over low heat, stirring often until the mixture thickens.
  10. Remove the pan from the heat and set aside to cool.
  11. In a separate bowl or stand mixer, combine the Crisco, butter, sugar, and ⅔ cup powdered sugar. Beat for about 15 minutes until the sugar dissolves and the mixture is light and fluffy.
  12. Add the vanilla and the cooled milk mixture. Beat well, then add the remaining powdered sugar until fully combined.
  13. Frost the cake once it has completely cooled. If using two round cake pans, remove the cakes by inverting the pans.
  14. Place one cake layer on a serving plate and frost the top. Place the second cake layer on top.
  15. Continue frosting the top and sides of the cake. Optional: Decorate the cake as desired.
