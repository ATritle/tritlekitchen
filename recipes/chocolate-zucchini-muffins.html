Chocolate Zucchini Muffins
minimalistbaker.com

Ingredients:

  • 2 TBSP flaxseed meal
  • 5 TBSP water
  • 1/4 cup applesauce
  • 1/4 cup maple or agave syrup
  • 1/3 cup coconut sugar
  • 1 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp sea salt
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil - melted
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 cup zucchini - finely shredded and squeezed
  • 3/4 cup flour - sifted
  • 1/3 cup oat flour
  • 1/3 cup almond flour
  • 1/3 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F and line a mini muffin tin, regular muffin tin, or bread pan with paper or silicone liners.
  2. In a small bowl, combine the flaxseed and water. Let sit for 5 minutes.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the applesauce, syrup, coconut sugar, baking soda, baking powder, sea salt, and cocoa powder until well mixed. Add the flaxseed mixture and stir well.
  4. Add the coconut oil and almond milk to the mixture and stir until fully incorporated.
  5. Lightly squeeze excess moisture from the shredded zucchini.
  6. Fold the zucchini into the batter until well combined. Then add the flour, oat flour, and almond flour and mix until incorporated.
  7. Fold in the chocolate chips and pour the batter into the prepared tin.
  8. Bake mini muffins for 12–15 minutes, regular muffins for 20–25 minutes, or a loaf in a bread pan for 45–60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  9. Store at room temperature for 3–4 days or freeze for up to 1–2 months.
