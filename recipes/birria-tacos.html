Birria Tacos

Ingredients:

  • 3-4 LBS chuck roast - 2" x 2" cubes
  • 3 large onions - 1 cut in half & 2 sliced
  • 4 Roma tomatoes - cut in half
  • 5 cloves garlic - mashed
  • 2 dried ancho chile peppers - seeds removed
  • 2 dried guajillo chile peppers - seeds removed
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 dashes ground cinnamon
  • 1 TBSP whole black peppercorns
  • 1 TBSP apple cider vinegar
  • water
  • salt - to taste
  • 1-2 TBSP cumin
  • 1 & 1/2 TBSP Better Than Bouillon Beef
  • 1-2 TBSP taco seasoning
  • 1-2 tsp dried oregano
  • 1-2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 red onion - diced
  • cilantro - chopped
  • flour tortillas
  • Chinuahua cheese
  • 1-2 limes

Directions:

    Directions:

    1. In a Dutch oven, add the halved onion, tomatoes, garlic, ancho peppers, guajillo peppers, bay leaf, cinnamon, salt, half of the cumin, peppercorns, apple cider vinegar, and enough water to cover the ingredients. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20-30 minutes, or until dark in color and fragrant.
    2. Remove the bay leaf. Strain the mixture, reserving 1 cup of the liquid. Add the solids and reserved liquid to a blender and blend until smooth.
    3. Line the bottom of a crockpot with sliced onions and place the 2" cubed chuck roast on top.
    4. Mix Better Than Bouillon with ¾ cup water and pour over the roast.
    5. Pour the blended mixture over the roast and sprinkle with cumin, taco seasoning, oregano, and garlic powder.
    6. Cover and cook on low for 8 hours, or until the beef is tender and easily shreds.
    7. Once cooked, remove the beef and shred. Strain the broth and transfer the consommé to a large bowl for dipping tortillas.
    8. Dip tortillas in the consommé, then pan-fry on both sides until lightly crispy and golden.
    9. Add Chihuahua cheese to one half of the tortilla and birria meat to the other half. Let the cheese melt.
    10. Fold the tortilla in half and flip to crisp both sides. Remove from pan and top with cilantro, diced red onion, and a squeeze of fresh lime juice.
