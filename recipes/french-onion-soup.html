French Onion Soup

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups onions - thinly sliced
  • 2 TBSP unsalted butter
  • 2 TBSP olive oil
  • 6 sprigs fresh thyme - leaves stripped
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 1½ cups grated Gruyère cheese
  • 2 TBSP Worcestershire sauce
  • pinch of salt & pepper

Directions:

  1. In a Dutch oven, heat the butter and olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Reduce heat to medium-low and add the sliced onions, thyme leaves, and bay leaf.
  3. Cook the onions until deeply caramelized, stirring every 10 minutes to prevent sticking. This process can take up to 45 minutes but is essential for developing rich flavor.
  4. Once the onions reach a deep caramel color, deglaze the pan with white wine. Increase heat to medium-high and bring to a boil, allowing the alcohol to cook off.
  5. Add the beef broth and return the soup to a boil. Reduce heat to low and let simmer.
  6. Ladle the soup into 4 oven-safe bowls. Top each bowl with toasted baguette slices.
  7. Divide the grated cheese evenly among the bowls, ensuring each has a generous amount on top.
  8. Place the bowls in the oven and bake until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  9. Optional – Toasted Baguette: Brush slices lightly with olive oil and sprinkle with garlic powder and Italian seasoning. Toast in the oven until golden.
